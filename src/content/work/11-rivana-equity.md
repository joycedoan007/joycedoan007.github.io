  <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">01 — Context</div>
  <h2 style="font: 600 22px/1.2 'Poppins', sans-serif; letter-spacing: -0.02em; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0;">The Challenge</h2>
</div>

Rivana Equity is a private equity firm that needed a professional online presence to communicate credibility to potential investors and operating partners. The challenge wasn't just visual — it was about translating an institutional-grade brand into a website that speaks to sophisticated investors without feeling cold or inaccessible.

The firm had strong fundamentals and an experienced team, but no web presence that matched. Deals and partnerships were happening through referrals and direct outreach. A proper website would give the brand legitimacy and a clear communication layer for both inbound investors and potential operating partners evaluating fit.

<div style="background: var(--bg-surface); border-left: 2px solid var(--primary-500); border-radius: 0 var(--radius-lg) var(--radius-lg) 0; padding: 20px 24px; margin: 28px 0 56px;">
  <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">My Role</div>
  <p style="font: 400 15px/26px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-primary); margin: 0;">I was one of three designers. While two handled screen-level UI execution, I owned the design foundation — information architecture, design system, Figma file structure, responsive standards, and component quality. My job was to build the system the team worked within, not just individual screens.</p>
</div>

<div style="margin: 0 0 20px;">
  <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">02 — Discovery</div>
  <h2 style="font: 600 22px/1.2 'Poppins', sans-serif; letter-spacing: -0.02em; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0;">Research & Key Insights</h2>
</div>

Investment firm websites operate on a different set of rules than consumer products. We looked at how investors and deal flow partners actually navigate these sites and identified four principles that shaped every decision.

<div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap: 12px; margin: 24px 0 56px;">
  <div style="background: var(--bg-surface); border: 1px solid var(--border-subtle); border-radius: var(--radius-xl); padding: 24px;">
    <div style="font: 600 11px/16px 'JetBrains Mono', monospace; color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 10px;">01</div>
    <p style="font: 600 14px/20px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 8px;">Trust before action</p>
    <p style="font: 400 13px/22px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-secondary); margin: 0;">Investors don't convert on CTAs — they convert on credibility. Team, track record, and investment thesis have to land before any contact prompt is relevant.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="background: var(--bg-surface); border: 1px solid var(--border-subtle); border-radius: var(--radius-xl); padding: 24px;">
    <div style="font: 600 11px/16px 'JetBrains Mono', monospace; color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 10px;">02</div>
    <p style="font: 600 14px/20px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 8px;">Clean hierarchy signals professionalism</p>
    <p style="font: 400 13px/22px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-secondary); margin: 0;">Heavy visual design feels "startup-y" in the finance space. Clear typography hierarchy and restrained aesthetics communicate institutional maturity.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="background: var(--bg-surface); border: 1px solid var(--border-subtle); border-radius: var(--radius-xl); padding: 24px;">
    <div style="font: 600 11px/16px 'JetBrains Mono', monospace; color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 10px;">03</div>
    <p style="font: 600 14px/20px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 8px;">Two distinct audiences, one site</p>
    <p style="font: 400 13px/22px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-secondary); margin: 0;">Potential investors and operating partners have different jobs to do on the site. The IA needed to serve both paths without making either feel secondary.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="background: var(--bg-surface); border: 1px solid var(--border-subtle); border-radius: var(--radius-xl); padding: 24px;">
    <div style="font: 600 11px/16px 'JetBrains Mono', monospace; color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 10px;">04</div>
    <p style="font: 600 14px/20px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 8px;">Desktop is the primary context</p>
    <p style="font: 400 13px/22px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-secondary); margin: 0;">Unlike consumer products, investment firm sites are primarily browsed on desktop — typically during due diligence. Mobile responsiveness matters, but the desktop experience is the primary canvas.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="margin: 0 0 20px;">
  <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">03 — Design</div>
  <h2 style="font: 600 22px/1.2 'Poppins', sans-serif; letter-spacing: -0.02em; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0;">Key Design Decisions</h2>
</div>

<div style="display: flex; flex-direction: column; gap: 10px; margin: 24px 0 56px;">
  <div style="border-left: 2px solid var(--primary-500); background: var(--bg-surface); border-radius: 0 var(--radius-xl) var(--radius-xl) 0; padding: 24px 28px;">
    <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">Decision 01</div>
    <p style="font: 600 15px/1.4 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 10px;">Team section as the trust anchor</p>
    <p style="font: 400 14px/24px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-primary); margin: 0;">Investors want to know who they're dealing with before they read about the fund's thesis. We placed the Team page high in the navigation and made partner profiles prominent — name, photo, background, and role clearly visible. This single decision shapes how the entire site feels to a first-time visitor.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="border-left: 2px solid var(--primary-500); background: var(--bg-surface); border-radius: 0 var(--radius-xl) var(--radius-xl) 0; padding: 24px 28px;">
    <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">Decision 02</div>
    <p style="font: 600 15px/1.4 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 10px;">Investment Criteria as a scannable checklist</p>
    <p style="font: 400 14px/24px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-primary); margin: 0;">Deal flow partners need to evaluate fit in under 30 seconds. Rather than long-form prose, we structured the Investment Criteria page as clear criteria blocks — sector focus, deal size, stage, and geography laid out so founders and brokers can self-qualify instantly without booking a call.</p>
  </div>
  <div style="border-left: 2px solid var(--primary-500); background: var(--bg-surface); border-radius: 0 var(--radius-xl) var(--radius-xl) 0; padding: 24px 28px;">
    <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">Decision 03</div>
    <p style="font: 600 15px/1.4 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0 0 10px;">Restrained visual language over decorative design</p>
    <p style="font: 400 14px/24px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-primary); margin: 0;">We made a deliberate call to keep the visual design minimal — controlled whitespace, a tight color palette, and typography doing most of the heavy lifting. In private equity, over-designed websites signal the wrong things. Restraint communicates confidence.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="margin: 48px 0 20px;">
  <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 8px;">06 — Outcome</div>
  <h2 style="font: 600 22px/1.2 'Poppins', sans-serif; letter-spacing: -0.02em; color: var(--text-bright); margin: 0;">Result</h2>
</div>

Rivana Equity launched with a website that matched the seriousness of their positioning — clean, credible, and immediately communicating who they are and what they invest in. The two-audience structure meant both investors and founders could find what they needed without friction.

The design system made the Webflow build straightforward and left the client with a site they could maintain and expand without design involvement.

<div style="background: var(--bg-surface); border: 1px solid var(--border-default); border-radius: var(--radius-xl); padding: 28px 32px; margin-top: 28px;">
  <div class="overline" style="color: var(--text-accent); margin-bottom: 12px;">Reflection</div>
  <p style="font: 400 15px/26px 'Poppins', sans-serif; color: var(--text-primary); margin: 0;">The biggest challenge on this project wasn't the design itself — it was balancing two audiences with different needs in a single, uncluttered navigation. In hindsight, I'd have pushed to do lightweight user testing with both an investor and a founder before finalizing the IA. Our assumptions about how each audience navigates turned out to be mostly right, but validated data would have made those calls with more confidence.</p>
</div>
